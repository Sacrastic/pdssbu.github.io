Public Distribution System

Ensuring food security for all citizens of India.

Overview

The Public Distribution System (PDS) is a nationwide food security system in India that distributes subsidized food and non-food items to India's poor. Established by the Government of India under the Ministry of Consumer Affairs, Food, and Public Distribution, PDS is a vital component of the country's safety net against hunger.

History of Public Distribution

PDS in 1960s

The public distribution of essential commodities was in existence in the country during the inter-war period. However, PDS, with its focus on the distribution of food grains in urban scarcity areas, had emanated from the critical food shortages of the 1960s. It contributed to containing food prices and ensured access to food for urban consumers. In the aftermath of the Green Revolution, PDS's outreach extended to tribal blocks and areas of high poverty in the 1970s and 1980s.

Revamped Public Distribution System (RPDS)

Launched in June 1992, RPDS aimed to strengthen and streamline the PDS, focusing on far-flung, hilly, and remote areas. It covered 1775 blocks where specific programs like the Drought Prone Area Programme (DPAP) and Integrated Tribal Development Projects (ITDP) were implemented.

Targeted Public Distribution System (TPDS)

Introduced in June 1997, TPDS aimed at providing food grains to the poor at subsidized prices. The scheme targeted 6 crore poor families, providing 20 kg of food grains per family per month. The number of Below Poverty Line (BPL) families was later increased, and the scheme was further enhanced to provide greater benefits.

Antyodaya Anna Yojana (AAY)

Launched in December 2000, AAY aimed to reduce hunger among the poorest segments of the BPL population by providing highly subsidized food grains. Initially covering one crore families, the scheme was later expanded to provide 35 kg of food grains per family per month. read more..

Our Mission

To ensure food security and reduce hunger by distributing essential commodities to the most vulnerable sections of society at subsidized prices.

Our Vision

To create a hunger-free India where every citizen has access to nutritious food, enabling them to lead a healthy and productive life.

Our Objectives

Ensure Food Security

Provide essential food grains and other commodities to the economically vulnerable sections of society at affordable prices.

Reduce Hunger

Alleviate hunger and malnutrition by providing adequate and nutritious food to all, especially during times of crisis.

Support Farmers

Ensure a stable market for farmers' produce by procuring food grains at Minimum Support Prices (MSP).
